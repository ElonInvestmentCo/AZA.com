# PAYVORA Wordmark Recommendations

## Direction

The PAYVORA wordmark should be a premium geometric sans-serif treatment derived from the approved primary logo. It should feel calm, stable, and technical without becoming cold or generic.

## Typography

- Base style: custom geometric sans-serif, closest production references are Inter Display, SF Pro Display, Satoshi, Aeonik, or Neue Haas Grotesk Display.
- Weight: 760-800 optical weight. Heavy enough to feel institutional, but not so bold that counters close at small sizes.
- Case: uppercase only for the official wordmark.
- Letterform character: wide, clean, low-contrast strokes with open counters and minimal ornament.
- Production note: convert the final wordmark to outlines before brand-master export so spacing and letter shape are locked across platforms.

## Customization Notes

- `P`: should visually echo the approved logo symbol with a confident vertical stem and open internal counter.
- `A`: keep the apex sharp and modern, with a balanced internal counter. Avoid overly decorative crossbars.
- `Y`: should feel stable, not playful; the fork should be slightly narrow to prevent the first syllable from feeling too wide.
- `V`: should be optically spaced wider than default because it creates strong negative space with `Y` and `O`.
- `O`: maintain a near-circular geometric form to reinforce trust and simplicity.
- `R`: keep the leg crisp and forward-moving, subtly matching the financial-flow idea in the primary symbol.

## Spacing

- Recommended tracking: `0` for the master wordmark, with manual kerning only.
- Critical pairs:
  - `PA`: slightly tighter than default.
  - `AY`: slightly tighter to prevent `PAY` from separating.
  - `YV`: slightly looser to avoid collision between diagonals.
  - `VO`: slightly tighter to keep `VORA` cohesive.
  - `RA`: slightly tighter for a strong closing rhythm.
- Wordmark clear space: minimum clear space should equal the height of the uppercase stem thickness multiplied by 4, or the height of the `P` counter when used in larger layouts.

## Proportions

- Overall feel: horizontally confident, not condensed.
- Ideal width-to-height ratio: approximately 4.3:1 to 4.5:1.
- Cap height: consistent across all letters.
- Baseline: flat and stable, with no descenders or decorative offsets.
- Stroke density: must remain readable at small UI-header sizes.

## Usage

- Primary color on light backgrounds: Deep Navy `#0B1B3A`.
- Reversed color on dark backgrounds: White `#FFFFFF`.
- Avoid gradients, shadows, outlines, bevels, and decorative effects.
- Use the wordmark alone only when brand recognition is already established or where the symbol would be visually redundant.
- Minimum digital width: 96px for the wordmark-only asset.
- Preferred UI header width: 120-160px depending on available space.
